# Assumption Taxonomy

Five categories of implicit assumptions found in dream seeds. Use these to classify
assumptions during Step 1 of the Reverse-Assumption method.

---

## [STRUCTURAL] — How things are organized

Assumptions about architecture, topology, and arrangement.

| Pattern | Example Assumption | Inversion Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Centralized | "There's a single source of truth" | Distributed, emergent consensus |
| Hierarchical | "Some components control others" | Flat, peer-to-peer, stigmergic |
| Modular | "Components are separable" | Entangled, holographic |
| Sequential pipeline | "Data flows A→B→C" | Parallel, circular, bidirectional |
| Container/contained | "X holds Y" | Y defines X, mutual containment |

**Signal words:** architecture, system, component, layer, module, pipeline, flow

---

## [TEMPORAL] — How things relate to time

Assumptions about sequence, duration, simultaneity, and reversibility.

| Pattern | Example Assumption | Inversion Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Linear progression | "Things get better over time" | Cyclical, oscillating, punctuated |
| Irreversibility | "Decisions are final" | Reversible, provisional, draft-mode |
| Synchronous | "Events happen in order" | Asynchronous, out-of-order, retroactive |
| Present-forward | "We plan for the future" | Future-back, starting from desired end |
| Duration matters | "Longer experience = more learning" | Intensity matters more than duration |

**Signal words:** before, after, sequence, schedule, cycle, history, future, evolve

---

## [CAUSAL] — What causes what

Assumptions about cause-effect relationships, dependencies, and influence.

| Pattern | Example Assumption | Inversion Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Direct causation | "A causes B" | B causes A, or A and B co-emerge |
| Single cause | "This happened because of X" | Multi-causal, overdetermined |
| Proportional | "Big causes → big effects" | Small causes → big effects (sensitivity) |
| Mechanistic | "We can trace the cause" | Emergent, no traceable cause |
| Input→Output | "Quality in → quality out" | Constraints in → creativity out |

**Signal words:** because, leads to, results in, depends on, triggers, causes

---

## [AGENTIAL] — Who or what has agency

Assumptions about who acts, who decides, and who/what is passive.

| Pattern | Example Assumption | Inversion Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Human agency | "Users decide, systems respond" | System initiates, user responds |
| Designer control | "We design the behavior" | Behavior emerges from use |
| Conscious choice | "Decisions are deliberate" | Decisions are habitual, environmental |
| Individual agent | "One entity acts" | Collective, distributed agency |
| Tool passivity | "Tools are inert until used" | Tools shape the user's thinking |

**Signal words:** user, agent, decide, control, choose, act, respond, design

---

## [SCALE] — At what level things operate

Assumptions about granularity, scope, and boundaries.

| Pattern | Example Assumption | Inversion Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed granularity | "We work at this level" | Zoom in/out, fractal self-similarity |
| Bounded system | "Clear inside/outside" | Permeable boundary, fuzzy edges |
| Single scale | "One level of organization" | Multi-scale, nested systems |
| Local effect | "Changes are contained" | Butterfly effects, non-local influence |
| Discrete units | "Things come in countable pieces" | Continuous, gradient, spectrum |

**Signal words:** level, scope, boundary, scale, size, individual, system, global
