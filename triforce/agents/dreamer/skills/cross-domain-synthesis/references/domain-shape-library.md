# Domain Shape Library

20 structural shapes with 3 donor domains each. Use these as starting points
for Step 2 of the Cross-Domain Synthesis method.

---

## 1. Competitive Allocation
**Shape:** Items compete for limited resources based on fitness criteria.
- **Ecology:** Species compete for niches; specialists outcompete generalists in stable environments
- **Economics:** Market competition drives price discovery and innovation
- **Immunology:** T-cells compete for antigen binding sites; best-fit clones proliferate

## 2. Feedback Amplification
**Shape:** Small signals get amplified through positive feedback loops.
- **Acoustics:** Microphone feedback — output becomes input, exponential growth
- **Epidemiology:** R₀ > 1 means each case creates more than one new case
- **Geology:** Erosion deepens channels, channeling more water, deepening further

## 3. Phase Transition
**Shape:** Gradual change accumulates until a sudden qualitative shift.
- **Physics:** Water→ice at 0°C — same molecules, entirely different behavior
- **Sociology:** Individual grievances → collective movement (threshold models)
- **Metallurgy:** Annealing — heating and slow cooling reorganizes crystal structure

## 4. Symbiotic Coupling
**Shape:** Two different systems co-evolve to mutual benefit.
- **Biology:** Mitochondria were once free bacteria; now inseparable from cells
- **Architecture:** Flying buttresses enabled thin walls enabled stained glass (structural↔aesthetic)
- **Linguistics:** Pidgin languages emerge when two language communities need to trade

## 5. Signal/Noise Separation
**Shape:** Distinguishing meaningful patterns from irrelevant variation.
- **Radio engineering:** Superheterodyne receiver — shift frequency to separate signal
- **Immunology:** Self vs non-self discrimination (thymic selection)
- **Archaeology:** Stratigraphy — layer position separates artifact from debris

## 6. Gradient Descent
**Shape:** Following local signals downhill to find optima.
- **Ant colonies:** Pheromone trails — stronger scent = better path
- **River formation:** Water follows gravity, carving channels of least resistance
- **Market pricing:** Arbitrage eliminates price differences across markets

## 7. Hierarchical Compression
**Shape:** Repeated patterns at one level become units at the next.
- **Music:** Notes→phrases→sections→movements→symphonies
- **Language:** Phonemes→morphemes→words→sentences→discourse
- **Biology:** Cells→tissues→organs→systems→organism

## 8. Distributed Consensus
**Shape:** Independent agents agree on shared state without central authority.
- **Crystallography:** Molecules self-organize into lattice without blueprint
- **Flocking:** Birds maintain formation through 3 local rules (separation, alignment, cohesion)
- **Common law:** Legal precedent emerges from accumulated individual decisions

## 9. Dormancy and Activation
**Shape:** Systems persist in inactive state until triggered.
- **Botany:** Seeds can remain dormant for centuries, germinating when conditions align
- **Volcanology:** Magma chambers build pressure over decades, erupt in hours
- **Immunology:** Memory B-cells persist for years, activate rapidly on re-exposure

## 10. Scaffolding and Removal
**Shape:** Temporary structure enables permanent construction, then is removed.
- **Construction:** Physical scaffolding around buildings during construction
- **Education:** Training wheels → removed when skill is internalized
- **Embryology:** Apoptosis — programmed cell death sculpts fingers from paddle-shaped limbs

## 11. Boundary Negotiation
**Shape:** The interface between systems is where interesting behavior emerges.
- **Ecology:** Ecotones (forest→meadow boundary) have highest biodiversity
- **Chemistry:** Catalysis happens at surfaces, not in bulk
- **Geopolitics:** Border regions develop hybrid cultures and trade languages

## 12. Periodic Renewal
**Shape:** Systems that periodically destroy and rebuild maintain long-term health.
- **Forestry:** Controlled burns prevent catastrophic wildfires; promote new growth
- **Economics:** Schumpeter's creative destruction — old firms fail, new ones emerge
- **Neuroscience:** Sleep prunes synapses, consolidating what matters

## 13. Exaptation
**Shape:** A feature evolved for one purpose gets repurposed for another.
- **Biology:** Feathers evolved for warmth, repurposed for flight
- **Technology:** SMS was a network diagnostic tool, repurposed for human messaging
- **Architecture:** Church buttresses became decorative features in Gothic revival

## 14. Resonance
**Shape:** Small periodic inputs at the right frequency produce large effects.
- **Physics:** Pushing a swing at its natural frequency builds amplitude
- **Music:** Sympathetic vibration — unstruck strings vibrate when nearby strings are played
- **Social:** Viral content resonates with existing emotional patterns

## 15. Encapsulation
**Shape:** Complex internals hidden behind simple interface.
- **Biology:** Cell membrane presents receptors; hides organelle complexity
- **Watchmaking:** Crown and hands interface; escapement mechanism hidden
- **Cooking:** A recipe is an encapsulation of chemistry, physics, and biology

## 16. Hysteresis
**Shape:** System state depends on history, not just current input.
- **Magnetism:** Iron retains magnetization after external field is removed
- **Economics:** Unemployment stays high even after recession ends (labor market scarring)
- **Psychology:** Trauma responses persist after the threatening stimulus is gone

## 17. Trophic Cascade
**Shape:** Removing/adding one element cascades through entire system.
- **Ecology:** Wolves reintroduced to Yellowstone → elk behavior changed → rivers changed course
- **Urban planning:** Removing a highway → neighborhood revival → property value shift → demographic change
- **Cooking:** One wrong spice doesn't just taste bad — it changes how all other flavors are perceived

## 18. Quorum Sensing
**Shape:** Individual behavior changes when group density crosses a threshold.
- **Microbiology:** Bacteria switch to biofilm formation when population density is sufficient
- **Sociology:** Bystander effect reverses when enough people intervene
- **Physics:** Bose-Einstein condensate — particles behave identically at critical density

## 19. Stigmergy
**Shape:** Agents coordinate through environmental modifications, not direct communication.
- **Termites:** Build complex mounds by following chemical traces in mud, no blueprint
- **Wikipedia:** Editors coordinate through the artifact (the article itself)
- **Trail formation:** Hikers create paths by walking; paths guide future hikers

## 20. Adaptive Radiation
**Shape:** Rapid diversification to fill available niches after a constraint is removed.
- **Evolution:** Mammals diversified rapidly after dinosaur extinction
- **Technology:** Smartphone apps exploded after the App Store removed distribution constraints
- **Cuisine:** Regional food traditions diversified when spice trade removed ingredient scarcity
